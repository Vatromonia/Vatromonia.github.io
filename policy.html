PRIVACY POLICY

We help our customers evolve their business, improve the way they do advertising and marketing, whether through social networks, websites. We believe that the responsible use of data supports business growth and builds strong brand-consumer relationships. As a company, we are committed to respecting and protecting the privacy of everyone we interact with. We undertake to be transparent in our management and processing of personal data at all times, in accordance with applicable privacy and data protection laws. This privacy notice explains in detail the types of personal data we may collect about you when you interact with us. Vatromonia is responsible for the processing of all personal data that you provide to us, including in connection with this website.

1.Information we may collect


The type of information we collect will depend on the circumstances and the service you use. In general, we will collect information about you and/or your use of our services as follows:

*Information about your use of the site

We collect information about how you use our website. This includes information about the pages you visit on our website, the services or information you are looking for, and the links and content you choose to access. We may also use the personal information you provide when you use our website to provide you with relevant content and to inform our marketing strategy. This type of activity is known as "profiling" and uses automated means to process your personal data to analyze or predict your personal preferences, interests or behaviours.

*Technical data

We collect information about the device or devices you use to access our site. This includes the collection of the unique mobile device identifier or Internet Protocol (IP) online identifiers, which are numbers that can uniquely identify a specific computer or other network device on the Internet. This information is linked to a cookie ID that we receive and process.

*Contact data

We collect your details when you sign up to receive email alerts, attend one of our events, download our content, subscribe to our newsletters or when you ask us to answer a question you have. The personal data we collect includes your name, email address, function and location.

*Enfants

We do not actively seek to collect information on children 16 years or younger. If you have concerns about your child’s privacy in connection with our services, or if you believe that your child may have entered personal data on our website, Please contact us at Contact@vatromonia.com We will delete this information from our records within a reasonable period of time.


2. How we use this information

Except as required by law, we use the personal data you provide for the following purposes:
-to provide the specific information or services you have requested
-to allow the download of our content
-vsend you newsletters and information about our brands and services
-respond to your requests and comments
-analyze and/or improve our services.
Under the Data Protection Act, we are obliged to advise you on the legal basis for the processing of your personal data. For the most part, the processing of your personal information is based on either a) our legitimate interests in providing you with the services you have requested or otherwise your customer relationship with us, or b) your consent, if any.

The categories of personal data that we collect under the CCPA are: personal information; geolocation data, professional or employment-related information; and other similar network activity.


3. How long will we keep your information

We will keep your personal data for as long as necessary for the relevant service, in accordance with our legal obligations. After this period, your personal data will either be securely deleted or made anonymous so that it can be used for analysis. You can request further information through the contact details provided in this Privacy Policy.


4. How we secure your information

We maintain appropriate organizational and technological protections to help protect against unauthorized use, access or accidental loss, alteration or destruction of personal data. We also seek to ensure that our service providers do the same.


5. Your rights

You object to the processing of your personal data when we rely on legitimate interests (or those of a third party) and you wish to object to the processing on this ground, because you consider that it has an impact on your fundamental rights and freedoms. You also have the right to object when we process your personal data for direct marketing or profiling purposes. You may object at any time and we will cease to process the information to which you have objected, unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds for further processing.
Access your personal data. If you make such a request and we hold personal data about you. We are required to provide you with information about this, including a description and a copy of the personal data and why we process it. We will ask you to prove your identity before granting access to your personal data. We will process your request within the time limits required by applicable law.
Request the transfer of your personal data. We will provide you or a third party you have chosen with your personal data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format. Please note that this right applies to personal data that you have provided to us; and whether we use your personal data on the basis of consent or when we have used the information to perform a contract with you.
Request the erasure (deletion) of your personal data. You have the right to ask us to delete or delete your data when you have successfully exercised your right to object (see above) or when we are required to delete your personal data to comply with local legislation. Please note that we may be required to retain certain information by law and/or for our own legitimate business purposes. But when we do, we will inform you.
Request the correction or update of your personal data. This allows you to have incomplete or inaccurate data we hold about you corrected.
Request the restriction of our processing of your personal data in certain situations. If you request it, we may continue to store your personal data but cannot process it as long as the restriction is in place.
Withdraw your consent. When you have given your consent to our processing of your personal data, you may withdraw your consent at any time. If you withdraw your consent, this will not affect the legality of what we did with your personal data before you withdraw your consent.
Complain. We will do our best to resolve any claim. However, if you believe that we have not resolved your complaint, you have the right to file a complaint with your local data protection authority.
If you exercise the above rights and there are questions about who you are, we may ask you to provide information from which we can ascertain your identity.


6. Our responsibility for links to websites

This privacy notice is limited to personal data collected by Vatromonia. We provide links within this site to other websites, including social media sites such as Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn. If you follow these links, you must use these sites in conjunction with their applicable user and privacy notices, as their data practices do not fall within the scope of this privacy notice. In addition, we may have no responsibility or control over the information collected by a third-party website and we may not be held responsible for the protection and confidentiality of the information that you may provide on such websites.

If you do not agree to the changes, please do not continue to use our services and refrain from sharing your personal data with us. You should check this notice frequently for updates.
